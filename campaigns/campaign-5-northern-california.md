## Why Northern California First

PureTask is launching in Northern California before anywhere else. This is Nathan's home market. The first cleaners, the first clients, the first reviews, the first proof points — they all come from here. This campaign isn't pretending to be national. It's proud to be local.

## Core Message

PureTask isn't starting in New York. It isn't starting in LA. It's starting here — in the Bay Area, in Sacramento, in Placer County, in the Sierra foothills. The first market to experience what the cleaning industry should have always been is Northern California. Your neighborhood. Your community. Be part of the first chapter.

## Audience Profile

**Who they are:**
- NorCal homeowners and renters aged 28–65
- Bay Area: Tech workers, dual-income households, high disposable income
- Sacramento/Placer: Families, suburban homeowners, value-conscious but quality-driven
- Foothills/Nevada County: Tight-knit community, word-of-mouth driven, trust-first
- All: Proud of where they live — local identity matters to them

**What they feel:**
- Local pride — they respond to "this is for us, not everyone"
- Community connection — they want to support local and be part of something local
- Practical — they have real homes that need cleaning
- Trust-forward — especially outside the Bay Area, local trust networks matter enormously

**What they respond to:**
- Specific local references (their city by name, their landmarks)
- "First in NorCal" exclusivity
- Community belonging — neighbors are doing this
- Local cleaners — people from their own area
- The combination of tech-forward AND locally grounded

### Week 1: PRIDE
**Theme:** "NorCal is where this starts. Not LA. Not NYC. Here."
**Job:** Make NorCal feel chosen and special. Create local ownership of the PureTask story.
**Tone:** Proud, warm, locally grounded

**Job:** Make PureTask feel local and accessible — not a faraway tech company, but people in their community.
**Tone:** Friendly, specific, neighborhood-level warmth
**Hook style:** "Background-checked PureTask cleaners are live in [City] right now."

### Week 3: COMMUNITY
**Theme:** "Your neighbors are already booking. Be part of what's building here."
**Job:** Social proof with local flavor. The NorCal PureTask community is forming.
**Tone:** Warm, inclusive, community pride
**Hook style:** "The first wave of PureTask clients in Sacramento is here."

### Week 4: OWNERSHIP
**Theme:** "You're not just a customer. You're part of the first chapter."
**Job:** Elevate early NorCal users into founding community members. History is being made here.
**Tone:** Inspiring, ownership-focused, forward-looking
**Hook style:** "The first market. The first clients. The first standard. That's NorCal."

## Visual Identity

### Art Style
Authentically Northern California. Real NorCal landscapes and neighborhoods — not generic suburbs. Specific, recognizable, proud. Warm California light throughout. Diverse communities. Feels local and specific, not like a national ad campaign dropped into the region.

### Color Palette
- Primary: PureTask Blue #0099FF
- Week 1: Golden California light — warm, proud, sunrise energy
- Week 2: Neighborhood warmth — green oak trees, suburban streets, community colors
- Week 3: Community warmth — diverse faces, warm afternoon light
- Week 4: Full color, forward-looking — bright NorCal sky blue merging with PureTask blue

### City-Specific Visual Requirements

| Market | Visual Landmark / Element Required |
|--------|-----------------------------------|
| Bay Area / SF | Bay Bridge, Golden Gate, SF skyline, Marin hills — VISIBLE through window or in background |
| Oakland / East Bay | Oakland hills, Lake Merritt area, distinctive Oakland neighborhoods |
| Sacramento | Capitol dome, Sacramento River, Tower Bridge — VISIBLE |
| Roseville / Rocklin | Suburban foothills, oak trees, newer construction homes |
| Auburn / Grass Valley | Historic downtown Main Street, pine trees, Gold Country character |
| Nevada City | Victorian architecture, pine forest, intimate small town feel |
| El Dorado Hills | Rolling hills, newer upscale suburban homes, wide streets |
| Truckee / Tahoe | Sierra Nevada peaks, pine trees, mountain town character |

### Every Image Must Have
- A recognizable NorCal element (landscape, landmark, neighborhood character)
- PureTask blue #0099FF element
- Warm California light (golden, natural, never harsh)
- Real NorCal people energy — diverse, real, not generic
- Local specificity — each city feels different from the others

## Local Targeting Notes

**Bay Area:**
- Higher income, tech-savvy, early adopter personality
- Price is less sensitive — quality and trust are the primary drivers
- LinkedIn performs strongly here
- Frame: "The tech you trust at work, now for your home"

**Sacramento County:**
- Family-focused, suburban, value-conscious
- Facebook performs extremely well
- Community and neighborhood language resonates
- Frame: "Your neighborhood. Your community. Real accountability."

**Placer County (Roseville/Rocklin/Lincoln):**
- Fast-growing, newer construction, higher income suburban
- Strong Facebook + Instagram presence
- Family and lifestyle content performs well
- Frame: "The new standard for NorCal's fastest-growing communities"

**Nevada County (Grass Valley/Nevada City):**
- Tight-knit, trust-first, word-of-mouth driven
- Community belonging is everything
- Smaller scale but high conversion if trust is established
- Frame: "Local. Verified. Accountable. For our community."
