## Core Message

The home cleaning industry hasn't meaningfully evolved since 2005. No GPS. No photo proof. No real accountability. Anonymous workers. Platforms that take 30–35% and offer nothing in return. Homeowners have accepted this as normal. Cleaners have accepted being underpaid as normal. PureTask rebuilt the entire thing from scratch — AI matching, background verification, GPS check-in, before & after photos, escrow payment, Reliability Scores, fair pay. This is what it looks like when you actually try to fix something.

---

## The Problem We're Solving

Nobody has challenged the cleaning industry's baseline assumptions:
- That you should just trust whoever shows up
- That a handful of star ratings is enough accountability
- That 30–35% platform fees are just the cost of doing business
- That "we're insured" is a sufficient trust signal
- That the client has no real power in the transaction

PureTask challenges every single one. This campaign makes that challenge public and bold.

## Audience Profile

**Who they are:**
- Widest possible net — any adult who has ever hired a cleaner or considered it
- People who've had bad experiences (no-shows, poor quality, no accountability)
- People who haven't tried cleaning services because they don't trust them
- People who are fine with their current service but haven't considered the alternative
- Both homeowners AND cleaners — this campaign speaks to both sides

**What they feel:**
- Resigned — "all cleaning services are the same"
- Frustrated (if burned before) — "I've tried this, it didn't work"
- Curious (if engaged) — "wait, there's a better way?"
- Inspired (by Week 4) — "I want to be part of this new standard"

**What they respond to:**
- Bold statements that challenge the status quo
- Direct comparisons — old way vs new way
- Technology as proof of progress (not gimmicky — substantive)
- A movement narrative — not just a product, a better standard

## Emotion Arc (Week by Week)

### Week 1: OUTRAGE
**Theme:** "The industry is a mess and you've experienced it."
**Job:** Name the problem boldly. Make people nod. Validate their frustration with the industry.
**Tone:** Confident, direct, a little angry on everyone's behalf
**Hook style:** "The cleaning industry hasn't changed in 20 years. Here's why that's a problem."

### Week 2: CONTRAST
**Theme:** "Here's what the old way looks like vs the PureTask way."
**Job:** Side-by-side comparisons that make the gap undeniable. Not subtle.
**Tone:** Clear, factual, let the contrast speak
**Hook style:** "Old standard: 4.8 stars from 12 reviews. New standard: Reliability Score 0–100 from every GPS check-in."

### Week 3: TECHNOLOGY
**Theme:** "This is what actually rebuilding looks like."
**Job:** Show the tech stack as proof of intent. AI matching, GPS, escrow, photos — this isn't a rebrand, it's a rebuild.
**Tone:** Innovative, substantive, confident
**Hook style:** "We didn't update the cleaning industry. We rebuilt it."

### Week 4: MOVEMENT
**Theme:** "You're not just booking a clean. You're choosing the new standard."
**Job:** Elevate the act of booking PureTask into a values statement. You're part of something.
**Tone:** Inspiring, forward-looking, inclusive
**Hook style:** "Every booking on PureTask is a vote for the new standard."

## Visual Identity

### Art Style
Bold, editorial, confident. This is a brand statement campaign — not soft lifestyle. Strong typography energy. Split screens (old vs new). Dark/light contrast. The visual arc mirrors the emotional arc — dark and frustrated in Week 1, bright and forward-looking by Week 4.

### Color Palette
- Primary: PureTask Blue #0099FF
- Week 1: Muted, desaturated, dark — the "broken" state. Grey, dim, frustrated tones.
- Week 2: Split — left side red/muted (old), right side blue/bright (new). Hard contrast.
- Week 3: Clean white, tech blue, precision — innovation and clarity.
- Week 4: Full color, warm and bright — the new standard has arrived.

### Image Direction Per Week

**Week 1 — The Broken State:**
A frustrated homeowner. A messy outcome after a supposed cleaning. Muted, dim tones. Real emotion — disappointment, resignation. The "this is what normal looks like" energy. NOT over-dramatic. Just real and recognizable.

**Week 2 — Old vs New:**
Direct split screen. Left side: muted/red tone — a generic cleaning platform UI, anonymous worker, star rating. Right side: PureTask blue/bright — GPS notification, Reliability Score, photo proof. Hard visual divide. Let the contrast do the talking.

**Week 3 — The Technology:**
Clean tech aesthetic. Phone screens showing GPS map, before/after photo interface, Reliability Score dashboard. Multiple screens in one frame if needed. White background, PureTask blue #0099FF dominant. Innovation energy without being cold.

**Week 4 — The Movement:**
Diverse group of homeowners AND cleaners together. Bright, warm, forward-looking. Multiple people, multiple backgrounds, united around a clean modern home. Spring light. Full PureTask branding. "We're all part of this" energy.

### Every Image Must Have
- Clear visual representation of the campaign week's theme
- PureTask blue #0099FF as dominant accent
- Real person energy — never stock
- Progressive brightness — each week slightly brighter/warmer than the last
- No fake stats, no fake reviews, no fake ratings
