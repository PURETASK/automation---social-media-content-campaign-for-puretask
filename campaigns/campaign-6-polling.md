## Why This Campaign Exists

This campaign serves two equally important purposes:

**1. Real Market Research**
PureTask is pre-launch. Every answer to every poll question is genuine intelligence:
- What price points are acceptable?
- What features matter most?
- What's the #1 trust barrier?
- What would make someone switch from their current service?
This data directly informs product decisions, pricing strategy, and future content.

**2. Algorithm Performance**
Poll and question posts are among the highest-performing formats on every social platform. They:
- Drive comments (which platforms reward heavily)
- Create saves and shares
- Build genuine community — people feel heard
- Generate remarketing audiences from engaged users
- Cost nothing to boost organically

## Core Message

We're building PureTask for you. Not for us. Not for investors. For the people who let strangers into their homes and the people who do that work. So we're asking. Pricing, services, trust, features — we want to know what you actually think. And we're listening.

## Audience Profile

**This campaign speaks to everyone — intentionally.**

The polling format is unique in that it doesn't require a specific audience profile. The question is the hook. Whether you're a homeowner, a cleaner, a parent, or an Airbnb host — if the question resonates, you answer.

**What all segments respond to:**
- Being asked their opinion (universal — everyone likes being consulted)
- Simple, clear questions that don't require much effort to answer
- Seeing what others think (social validation)
- Feeling like their answer will actually be used

## Emotion Arc (Week by Week)

### Week 1: PRICING
**Theme:** "What would you actually pay?"
**Job:** Understand price sensitivity across all audience segments. Validate or challenge PureTask's current pricing model.
**Tone:** Curious, non-salesy, genuinely asking
**Hook style:** "Quick question for homeowners: what's a fair price for a verified cleaner?"

### Week 2: SERVICES
**Theme:** "What matters most to you in a home cleaning service?"
**Job:** Understand feature prioritization. What do clients actually care about vs what platforms assume they care about?
**Tone:** Conversational, practical, relatable
**Hook style:** "If you could only have one thing, what would it be?"

### Week 3: TRUST
**Theme:** "What would actually make you feel safe booking online?"
**Job:** Map the trust barriers. Understand exactly what prevents booking and what would remove that barrier.
**Tone:** Empathetic, honest, safe to answer
**Hook style:** "Be honest — what's the real reason you haven't booked yet?"

### Week 4: PLATFORM & FEATURES
**Theme:** "What would make you switch from your current service?"
**Job:** Competitive intelligence. What's the gap between what people have and what they'd move for?
**Tone:** Direct, practical, forward-looking
**Hook style:** "What would it actually take for you to try something new?"

## Full Poll Content Plan (20 Polls)

### Week 1 — Pricing (Posts 1–5)

**Poll 1: Hourly Rate Tolerance**
Question: "What's a fair hourly rate for a verified, background-checked home cleaner?"
Options: Under $25/hr · $25–35/hr · $35–50/hr · $50+/hr
Why we're asking: Validates Bronze–Platinum pricing tiers
Platform note: Works on all platforms. LinkedIn skews higher. TikTok skews lower.

**Poll 2: Reliability Premium**
Question: "Would you pay more for a cleaner with a 95/100 Reliability Score vs an unknown?"
Options: Yes, easily · Yes, a little more · No · Depends on how much more
Why we're asking: Tests willingness to pay for verified quality
Platform note: Strong on Facebook and LinkedIn

**Poll 3: No-Show Guarantee**
Question: "If a cleaner no-showed, what's fair compensation from the platform?"
Options: $25 credit · $50 cash back · Full refund only · Full refund + priority rebooking
Why we're asking: Validates PureTask's $50 no-show bonus policy
Platform note: High engagement — people have strong opinions here

**Poll 4: Pricing Model**
Question: "Do you prefer hourly or flat-rate pricing for home cleaning?"
Options: Hourly · Flat rate · Either works · Depends on the job
Why we're asking: Informs pricing structure decisions
Platform note: Great for Facebook groups and neighborhood communities

**Poll 5: Current Spend**
Question: "What's your current monthly spend on professional home cleaning?"
Options: $0 (don't use it) · Under $100 · $100–200 · $200+
Why we're asking: Market sizing + understanding current customer vs non-customer split
Platform note: Instagram stories + Facebook. Sensitive — frame carefully.

### Week 2 — Services (Posts 6–10)

**Poll 6: #1 Pain Point**
Question: "What's the #1 thing you wish your cleaner did better?"
Options: Communication · Consistency · Thoroughness · Punctuality
Why we're asking: Feature prioritization — what drives dissatisfaction
Platform note: High engagement — people love to vent about this

**Poll 7: Photo Proof Value**
Question: "Would you pay extra for before & after photos on every clean?"
Options: Yes, absolutely · Maybe · No · I'd expect this for free
Why we're asking: Tests photo proof as a premium vs baseline feature
Platform note: Instagram — very visual audience

**Poll 8: Same Cleaner**
Question: "How important is it to always get the SAME cleaner?"
Options: Essential — deal breaker · Very important · Nice to have · Doesn't matter
Why we're asking: Validates the "favourite cleaner" rebooking feature priority
Platform note: High relatability — seniors and families especially

**Poll 9: Cleaning Frequency**
Question: "How often do you get your home professionally cleaned?"
Options: Weekly · Bi-weekly · Monthly · Rarely or never
Why we're asking: Market frequency data — understanding booking cadence
Platform note: All platforms. Good for retargeting non-users.

**Poll 10: Biggest Pain Point**
Question: "What's your biggest home cleaning challenge right now?"
Options: Deep cleaning keeps falling behind · Hard to find reliable help · Too expensive · Not enough time to even book
Why we're asking: Surfaces the real barrier — is it cost, trust, or time?
Platform note: Facebook and Threads — conversational

---

### Week 3 — Trust (Posts 11–15)

**Poll 11: Trust Drivers**
Question: "What would make you most comfortable letting a stranger clean your home?"
Options: Background check · GPS tracking · Before/after photos · Verified reviews
Why we're asking: Feature priority ranking for trust stack
Platform note: Extremely high engagement. Facebook families + adult children especially.

**Poll 12: Bad Experience**
Question: "Have you ever had a bad experience with a cleaning service?"
Options: Yes — never went back · Yes — gave another chance · No bad experiences · Haven't used one
Why we're asking: Quantifies the trust deficit in the market
Platform note: Very high engagement. People love to share bad experiences.

**Poll 13: Escrow Reaction**
Question: "Would you trust a cleaning platform more if payment was held in escrow until YOU approve the work?"
Options: Yes — game changer · Yes, somewhat · Doesn't really matter · What's escrow?
Why we're asking: Tests escrow as a trust signal AND educates on the feature
Platform note: LinkedIn + Facebook. Needs brief escrow explanation in caption.

**Poll 14: Profile Trust**
Question: "How much does a cleaner's profile photo affect your trust?"
Options: A lot — I look carefully · Somewhat · Not much · Not at all
Why we're asking: ID verification and profile completeness priority
Platform note: Instagram and TikTok — visual platforms where this resonates

**Poll 15: GPS Trust**
Question: "Would GPS arrival confirmation make you more likely to book a cleaning service?"
Options: Definitely — I'd love this · Probably yes · No difference · I'd find it too intrusive
Why we're asking: Tests GPS as conversion driver vs privacy concern
Platform note: All platforms. Adult children + families respond strongly.

### Week 4 — Platform & Features (Posts 16–20)

**Poll 16: Booking Barrier**
Question: "What's the #1 reason you haven't booked home cleaning recently?"
Options: Too expensive · Don't trust strangers · Can't find reliable help · Don't need it right now
Why we're asking: Direct barrier identification for conversion optimization
Platform note: Highest strategic value poll — run on all platforms with budget

**Poll 17: Founding Member Appeal**
Question: "Would 'founding member' locked pricing make you sign up for a new platform faster?"
Options: Yes — I'd sign up today · Maybe · No · What's a founding member?
Why we're asking: Tests founding member concept before Campaign 2 launches
Platform note: Works well before or during Founding Member campaign

**Poll 18: Feature Ranking**
Question: "Which platform feature matters MOST to you?"
Options: Background checks · Before/after photos · Same cleaner every time · Escrow payment
Why we're asking: Final feature priority ranking — informs product roadmap
Platform note: All platforms. High saves/shares.

**Poll 19: Booking Method**
Question: "How would you prefer to book a home cleaning service?"
Options: App · Website · Text/WhatsApp · Phone call
Why we're asking: UX/channel preference data — informs booking flow design
Platform note: TikTok + Instagram for younger audience. Facebook for older.

**Poll 20: Cleaner Fairness**
Question: "Did you know most cleaning platforms keep 30–35% of what cleaners earn?"
Options: Yes, I knew this · No, that seems high · No — but I don't mind · No — I'd pay more if they kept it
Why we're asking: Tests awareness of fair pay angle AND empathy for cleaners
Platform note: Strong on LinkedIn and Facebook. Bridges client + cleaner campaigns.

## Visual Identity

### Art Style
Clean, conversational, social-native. Genuine question energy — not a survey, not a form, not corporate research. Feels like a real person asking. Bright, simple graphics. Question mark and dialogue bubble imagery. PureTask blue #0099FF primary.

### Color Palette
- Primary: PureTask Blue #0099FF
- Week 1: Green and blue — money, value, financial themes
- Week 2: Warm orange and blue — home, service, practical
- Week 3: Deep blue and white — trust, security, safety
- Week 4: Full blue — platform confidence, forward-looking

### Image Direction Per Post

Each poll post has a simple, clear visual that relates to the question topic:

- **Pricing polls:** A clean graphic with the question text large, money/calculator iconography, PureTask blue background
- **Service polls:** A lifestyle image of a clean home with the poll question overlaid
- **Trust polls:** A trust-signal graphic (badge, shield, checkmark) with the question
- **Feature polls:** Phone mockup showing the relevant PureTask feature with the question overlaid

### Caption Formula (Same Every Post)
1. Opening line: The question, stated plainly
2. Brief context: Why this matters / why we're asking
3. The poll options (listed in caption for platforms without native polls)
4. Closing: "Tell us below ↓ Your answer helps us build this right."
5. URL: https://www.puretask.co (always present)

### Every Image Must Have
- The question text clearly readable
- PureTask blue #0099FF dominant
- Simple, uncluttered design — the question IS the content
- No fake stats, no fake response counts shown

## Data Collection Plan

After each poll closes:
1. Record results in MarketResearch entity with exact percentages
2. Note platform-specific variation (LinkedIn vs TikTok audiences differ)
3. Tag with `Polling-v1` and the specific question topic
4. Flag any result that contradicts PureTask's current assumptions
5. Use insights to brief next content generation batch

**Most strategically important polls:**
1. Poll 16 (Booking Barrier) — direct conversion intelligence
2. Poll 11 (Trust Drivers) — feature priority confirmation
3. Poll 18 (Feature Ranking) — product roadmap input
4. Poll 20 (Cleaner Fairness) — bridges both audience sides
